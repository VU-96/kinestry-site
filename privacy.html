The fine print, kept short

Privacy Policy

Last updated · 23 June 2026
Kinestry collects no data. Everything you put into the app — names, dates, photos, notes — stays on your device. We never see it.

What the app stores

Your family tree (people, relationships, dates, places, notes, photos) is stored in a local database on your phone. Backups you create are saved as files on your phone. Nothing is uploaded anywhere. Kinestry has no servers, no accounts, no sign-in, no analytics, no advertising and no trackers of any kind.

Internet access

Kinestry requests the network permission for exactly one feature: Desktop Mode, which lets a laptop browser on your own Wi-Fi network view and edit your tree while your phone is present. This traffic flows directly between your phone and your laptop on your local network. It never touches the internet, and it stops the moment you turn Desktop Mode off. Pairing requires a code shown on your phone and your explicit approval of each device.

Outbound links

The "Visit Website" and "Privacy Policy" buttons in Settings open this site in your phone's own web browser. Kinestry itself sends nothing — your browser makes that request, exactly as it would for any link you tap. We receive no information about you when you do this.

Calendar

If you tap "Add birthday" or "Add memorial day" on a person, Kinestry hands that one date and name to your phone's own calendar app to create the event — a local action, nothing is sent anywhere. On Desktop Mode (the browser version), the same button instead opens Google Calendar's website with that name and date filled in, since a browser has no device calendar to hand it to. That's the only case where something you choose to add leaves your device, and only because you tapped that specific button — exactly like the outbound links below.

Photos

If you attach photos to people in your tree, the app asks for photo access and keeps a copy inside its own private storage. Photos are never uploaded.

Notifications

The only notification Kinestry shows is the persistent "Desktop Mode is on" notice while your phone is serving your tree to your laptop — required by Android so you always know it is running.

Sharing and exports

When you export a PDF, image, GEDCOM or backup file, you choose where it goes using your phone's standard share dialog. What you share, and with whom, is entirely in your hands.

Children

Kinestry does not collect data from anyone, including children. Family members you record in your tree exist only on your device.

Changes

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be published at this address and the date above will change. Since Kinestry's design principle is that your data never leaves your device, do not expect much to change.
